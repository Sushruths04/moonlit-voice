# DreamVoice — Recording Guide

Read this script aloud when recording your voice clip. Speak slowly, warmly, and clearly —
like you're telling a bedtime story to a 6-year-old.

---

## What to say (read all of this):

> Once upon a time, there was a little bear named Luna. Luna loved to explore the
> forest near her cozy home. One evening, she found a tiny golden star stuck in the
> mud by the river. Very gently, she picked it up and held it close. The star began
> to glow, warm and soft, like a little nightlight. Luna whispered, "Don't worry,
> little star. I'll keep you safe tonight." She carried the star back to her den,
> curled up with it, and closed her eyes. The forest grew quiet. The moon smiled
> down through the trees. And Luna drifted off to sleep, holding the warmest star
> in the whole wide world. Goodnight, Luna. Sweet dreams.

---

## Tips for a good recording:

1. **Find a quiet room.** Turn off fans, AC, TV. No background noise.
2. **Speak 5–30 seconds minimum.** The longer, the better the voice clone.
3. **Use your normal voice.** Don't whisper or shout — just speak naturally and warmly.
4. **Hold the mic 6–12 inches from your mouth.** Not too close (pops), not too far (thin).
5. **No music or effects.** Just your raw voice.
6. **You can read the script above**, or tell any short story in your own words — as long
   as it's clear, calm, and in the language you want the app to narrate in.
7. **Supported languages for voice cloning:** English, Hindi, and 28 others (Arabic,
   Chinese, French, German, Japanese, Korean, Spanish, etc.). **Kannada is NOT supported**
   natively — it requires a fine-tuned model (experimental, post-MVP).
